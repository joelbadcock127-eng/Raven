Devonport & the North-West

Only 30 minutes from Ten Fifty Bakers, Devonport is the gateway to Tasmania’s North-West Coast. Devonport won a Silver Award at the 2025 Australia’s Top Tourism Town Awards and was named the Gold Winner in 2024. It’s also home to the Spirit of Tasmania terminal and daily flights to Melbourne, perfect for seamless access to and from the island.

The surrounding coast is rich with artisan producers, beaches, and natural attractions, all within an easy drive of the retreat.

Ghost Rock Vineyard

Coastal cool-climate wines and a relaxed cellar-door restaurant.

A charming creative hub where streets and laneways are transformed into an open-air art gallery celebrating Tasmania’s history and landscape.

Launceston & the Tamar Valley

Just an hour from the retreat, Launceston combines heritage charm with a globally celebrated food and wine culture. Winding north from the city, the Tamar Valley Wine Trail meanders through riverbanks and hillsides to the coast at Beauty Point, perfect for a day of tastings, cellar doors, and regional flavours.

Highlights Include

Sinapius Vineyard

Small-batch pinot noir and chardonnay at Pipers River.

Cataract Gorge Reserve

Launceston’s spectacular riverside gorge, ideal for a morning walk or picnic.

Seahorse World & Platypus House

Unique, side-by-side attractions in Beauty Point, dedicated to marine and native Tasmanian wildlife.

Tasmania Zoo

Home to Tasmanian devils and a wide variety of native and exotic species, perfect for families and wildlife lovers.

Brady’s Lookout

A classic Tamar Valley vantage point near Exeter, overlooking the river and surrounding vineyards.

Explore on Horseback

For those seeking adventure without the drive, Cradle Country Adventures guided horse rides right on Bakers Beach, just minutes from Ten Fifty Bakers.

Ride along the sand dunes and shoreline with views across Narawntapu National Park, a rare chance to experience Tasmania’s wilderness from a new perspective.

Suitable for all experience levels, it’s a memorable way to connect with the landscape that surrounds your stay.

Explore on Foot

For those craving to move slowly through wilderness, Bakers Walking Co offers guided walking tours departing directly from Ten Fifty Bakers.

Wander along private tracks, hidden creeks, and bushland fringes, with sweeping views across Narawntapu’s rugged coastline.

Every step brings you deeper into Tasmania’s silent beauty, ideal for all fitness levels, with time to breathe, listen, and reconnect. It’s a walk that lingers long after the horizon fades.
